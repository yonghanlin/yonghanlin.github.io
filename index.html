Hi, I am Yong-Han Lin, you can call me Johanne or in Chinese: 林詠涵. I am currently a research assistant located in Taiwan, working with Dr. Yung-Ju (Stanley) Chang at the Mobile Ubiquitous Interaction (MUI) Lab. I received the degree of B.S. in Computer Science in the summer of 2022 from the National Yang-Ming Chiao-Tung University (former: National Chiao Tung University).

My research interests lie in the fields of Human-Computer Interaction (HCI) and Ubiquitous Computing (UbiComp). I am interested in investigating the well-being challenges in people’s daily interactions with ubiquitous technologies and create systems to solve the problems. Recently, my research has focused on investigating and evaluating users' experiences with the intelligent notification system we developed to manage notifications effectively and efficiently. As a HCI researcher, I envision a future in which all technologies understand people and support their well-being.

In the past, I had two remote research internship experiences. One was at the UC Davis Collaborative and Social Computing (CSC) Lab, where I worked with Dr. Hao-Chuan Wang and Dr. Yi-Chieh (EJ) Lee. The other was at the Polytechnique Montréal Human-Centered Design (HCD) Lab, where I worked with Dr. Jinghui Cheng. Also, I was a software engineering intern at Xaduro Inc., a travel tech startup in Taiwan.
